The rapid integration of Generative AI within regulated sectors has outpaced the development of robust governance frameworks, creating a critical accountability gap. As organizations rush to automate complex decision-making processes, the risk of algorithmic bias and data opacity increases exponentially. For institutions operating in sensitive domains, the challenge is not merely technical but inherently socio-technical, requiring a shift from reactive compliance to proactive ethical design.

Effective AI governance must be anchored in transparency and human-centric accountability. To mitigate the systemic failures often observed in digital transformation projects, specialists must bridge the gap between high-level policy and engineering implementation. By prioritizing ethical audits and stakeholder impact assessments, we can ensure that AI serves as a tool for institutional integrity rather than a liability to social trust.
